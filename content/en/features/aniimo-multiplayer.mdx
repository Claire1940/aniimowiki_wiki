<Section icon={Swords} title="Competitive Play: Diamond Egg Heist and PvP">

The pinnacle of competitive **aniimo multiplayer** is the **Diamond Egg Heist**. Set in the treacherous Lost Isles, this mode pits six teams of three against each other in a race to claim a Diamond Egg. This isn't just a simple capture-the-flag variant; it requires a deep understanding of movement abilities and team composition.

In this mode, players must navigate the terrain using Twine abilities—gliding, climbing, and swimming—to reach the central objective. Once a team secures the egg, the carrier becomes a high-profile target. The remaining two teammates must provide protection, using defensive Aniimo like Nimbi to shield the carrier or aggressive types like Emberpup to intercept pursuers.

| Feature | Diamond Egg Heist | Holo-Battle Interlink |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Player Count** | 18 Players (6 Teams of 3) | 1-3 Players |
| **Primary Goal** | Secure and escort the Diamond Egg | Defeat waves of enemies/bosses |
| **Map Location** | Lost Isles | Polaris Institute |
| **Key Rewards** | Darkler-form Aniimo, Rare Eggs | Upgrade materials, Resonators |
| **Combat Style** | PvEvP (Creatures + Players) | PvE (Simulation) |

<Callout type="warning" title="Strategy Alert">
In Diamond Egg Heist, over-extending without your team is the fastest way to lose. Always ensure at least one player in your trio has a mobility-focused Aniimo to act as a scout or emergency carrier.
</Callout>

Beyond the Heist, the **Holo-Battle Interlink** offers a more structured competitive environment. While primarily focused on clearing simulations, the leaderboards and score-chasing aspects provide a platform for players to showcase their combat efficiency against the community.

<Section icon={Zap} title="Cooperative Challenges: Alpha Bosses and Sanctums">

For those who prefer collaboration over competition, **aniimo multiplayer** offers extensive PvE content. The continent of Idyll is home to **Alpha Aniimo**, massive versions of standard creatures with significantly higher health pools and devastating attack patterns. These encounters, such as the water-themed Bubbask or the formidable Minespine, often require a full party of Pathfinders to manage aggro and apply enough Break damage to create openings.

**Sanctum Puzzles** represent a different kind of cooperation. These are dedicated dungeons, like the *Sanctum: Rigged Maze*, designed for two-player co-op. Players must use their Aniimo's unique environmental abilities—such as using a Fire type to power a steam engine while a Wind type navigates a floating platform—to reach the end.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Form a Party">
    Use the social menu (Esc > Social) to invite friends or use the matchmaking kiosks in Astra to find a partner.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Select Complementary Roster">
    Ensure your team covers multiple elements. If your partner brings a high-DPS Fire type, consider bringing a Support Wind type or a Rock type for BREAK pressure.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Enter the Encounter">
    Travel to the Alpha Boss icon on the map or enter a Sanctum portal. All party members must be present to trigger the start.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Coordinate Twine Usage">
    Communicate when you are switching to Twine Mode. Direct control is essential for dodging boss "ultimate" attacks and executing combos.
  </Step>
</Steps>
