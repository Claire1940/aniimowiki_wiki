<Section icon={Shield} title="Mastering the Break Gauge and Stagger Mechanics">

The most critical mechanic in **aniimo pvp** is the **Break Gauge**. This gauge acts as a shield; as long as it is active, your Aniimo can withstand significant pressure. However, once this gauge is depleted, your character enters a staggered state, leaving them vulnerable to "large damage" combos and Ultimates. Savage players will exploit a broken guard immediately, often ending a match in a single well-timed sequence.

Managing your own gauge is just as important as depleting your opponent's. Defensive play, such as well-timed dodges and blocks, can preserve your gauge, while aggressive "poking" can slowly chip away at the enemy's defenses. If you find your gauge low, it is often better to tag out and let it regenerate safely on the sidelines.

| Resource | Primary Function | Recovery Method |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Health Points** | Total survivability | Healers (Glacy) or passive items |
| **Break Gauge** | Posture and stagger protection | Regenerates while not taking hits |
| **Energy/Skill** | Powers special abilities | Successful hits or time-based regen |
| **Ultimate Charge** | Game-ending powerful moves | Dealing damage and taking hits |

<Section icon={Target} title="Advanced Tactics: Tagging and Ultimate Timing">

In high-level **aniimo pvp**, the difference between a veteran and a novice is how they handle character swaps. Tagging out has a specific cooldown. If you swap characters recklessly, you may find yourself stuck with a low-health unit with no way to escape. Efficient players use the tag system to "dodge" incoming Ultimates or to bring in a fresh Break Gauge when theirs is about to snap.

Timing your Ultimate is the second pillar of advanced play. Because most Ultimates have a significant "wind-up" period, they are easily dodged in neutral play. The optimal strategy is to wait for a **Guard Break**. Once the opponent is staggered, they cannot move, making it the perfect window to unleash your most powerful attack.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="The Poke Phase">
    Use fast, low-commitment attacks to chip away at the opponent's **Break Gauge** while monitoring your own.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="The Guard Break">
    Once the gauge is nearly empty, commit to a heavy combo to trigger the stagger state.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="The Ultimate Finish">
    Immediately trigger your Ultimate while the opponent is staggered. This ensures a 100% hit rate.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="The Reset">
    If the opponent survives, tag out to a healer or shielder to recover while your DPS unit's cooldowns reset.
  </Step>
</Steps>
