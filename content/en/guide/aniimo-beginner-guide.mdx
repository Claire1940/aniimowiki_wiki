<Section icon={Map} title="An Introduction to Twining and Control Mechanics">

Getting started in the world of Aniimo requires understanding how to interact with your creatures directly. While the game allows you to send your companion into battle while you watch from the sidelines, manual control is the key to conquering difficult encounters. 

This manual control system is called **Twining**. When you twin with an Aniimo, you assume direct control over its movement and attacks. However, every creature features distinct movement speeds, attack animations, and recovery frames. Testing a creature's feel before committing your valuable upgrade materials is highly recommended.

**Video Highlights:**
- **Twining Importance**: Direct control is far more effective than automated sidelines observation.
- **Playstyle Fit**: Different creatures have unique movement speeds and attack animations.
- **Resource Management**: Test-drive your creatures to ensure you enjoy their flow before upgrading.

<Section icon={Swords} title="Early Game Team Building and Elemental Matchups">

Constructing a balanced team early on will save you hours of grinding. The game features a rock-paper-scissors elemental system where holding an elemental advantage is critical. To progress smoothly through the Hollow Battle Sim, you should focus on building three primary damage dealers (DPS) that cover the core elemental triangle.

Focusing on Grass, Water, and Fire DPS units allows you to challenge various levels of the simulator to farm essential held items and runes.

| Element | Strong Against | Weak Against | Primary Role in Team |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Fire** | Grass | Water | Main DPS / Shield Breaker |
| **Water** | Fire | Grass | DPS / Crowd Control |
| **Grass** | Water | Fire | DPS / Utility Support |

Prioritizing your DPS and Break units is highly efficient because they spend the most time active on the battlefield. Most support and healer units do not require heavy early-game investment. Their buffs are often static, meaning their utility remains high even at lower levels.

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="The Resonance System and Stat Customization">

To push your creatures past their initial limits, you must utilize the Resonance system. This mechanic functions similarly to character ascension in other RPGs, allowing your creatures to progress through distinct growth stages.

To advance your Resonance stages, you must gather two main resources:
1. **Species-Specific Crystals**: Obtained by catching duplicates of the same Aniimo species in the wild.
2. **Astronite Shards**: Earned by spending your daily energy to open boss chests scattered across the world.

| Resource Type | Primary Source | Usage |
|---|---|---|
| **Species Crystals** | Catching Duplicates | Unlocking Resonance Nodes |
| **Astronite Shards** | Daily Energy Boss Chests | Ascending Resonance Stages |

Resonance does more than provide basic stat boosts; it awards **Capability Points**. These points act like specialized effort values, allowing you to customize your creature's stats to fit your specific tactical needs.

<Callout type="success" title="Growth Success">
Maxing out Resonance on a lower-tier creature with great traits can often make it outperform a base-level rare creature.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Target} title="Avoiding the Hyper-Investment Trap">

It is incredibly easy to fall into the trap of hyper-investing in the very first creatures you capture. While leveling and resonance are straightforward, deeper systems like **Great Potentials**, **Personalities**, and **Traits** dictate a creature's true ceiling.

These attributes are locked the moment you capture an Aniimo. Investing heavily in a creature with poor potential or an incompatible personality will waste rare resources that are difficult to recover.

  <Step num="3" title="Hunt High Potentials">

  <Step num="4" title="Commit Deep Resources">

  </Step>

</Callout>

<Section icon={Shield} title="Utilizing the Homeland and Crafting Systems">

Do not ignore the Homeland housing and farming system. Beyond offering a relaxing break from combat, the Homeland serves as a passive source of power. 

By completing Homeland requests, you earn specialized materials to craft unique RV decorations. These decorations provide passive stat boosts to specific elements across your entire team.

| Decoration Type | Crafting Material | Passive Benefit |
|---|---|---|
| **Fire RV Decor** | Overworld Collectibles | Boosts Fire Element Damage |
| **Water RV Decor** | Homeland Request Rewards | Boosts Water Element Defense |
| **Grass RV Decor** | Rare Plant Harvests | Boosts Grass Element Utility |

<Callout type="warning" title="Keep Your Loot">
Never sell overworld collectibles to general vendors. Keep them in your storage, as they are frequently required for high-yield Homeland requests.
</Callout>

</Section>
