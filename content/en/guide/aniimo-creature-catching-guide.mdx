<Section icon={Clock} title="Environmental Triggers and Time Manipulation">

Spawns in Aniimo are rarely static. Most creatures are tied to environmental triggers that must be met before they manifest in the world. The most common trigger is the time of day. For instance, the normal form of Ignis only appears during the night. If you arrive at a spawn location during the day, the area will appear empty, or only "baby" versions of other creatures will be present.

Fortunately, the game allows for manual time manipulation. By accessing the "Day and Night" toggle in your menu, you can speed up time to jump to the required period. This is vital for completing your collection efficiently. However, be aware that changing time does not always clear current combat status, and some Anima may "run up" on you during the transition if you are standing too close to a spawn node.

| Trigger Type | Examples | Catching Strategy |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Time-Based** | Night only, Dawn only | Use the Time Jump tool to skip wait times |
| **Weather-Based** | Rainy, Foggy, Sunny | Check the weather icon next to the mini-map |
| **Chain-Based** | Catch 15 Highland forms | Clear inventory for a long catching session |
| **Action-Based** | Defeat X students | Focus on combat before the main spawn appears |

<Callout type="warning" title="Weather Deception">
Be careful of "fake" night. Sometimes it may be raining during the day, making the sky dark enough to look like night, but the sun is still technically up behind the clouds. Always verify the actual game time in the menu before searching for nocturnal Anima.
</Callout>
