### Primary Attribute Breakdown

Stats in Aniimo are streamlined but offer significant depth when paired with the skill tree. Unlike traditional RPGs that separate physical and magical power, the "Attack" stat in Aniimo governs the damage output for all skill types, simplifying the gearing process while emphasizing role specialization.

| Stat | Function | Impact on Combat |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **HP** | Health Points | Determines total survivability before fainting |
| **Attack** | Offensive Power | Increases damage for both physical and magical skills |
| **Phys. Def** | Physical Defense | Reduces damage taken from melee and ranged strikes |
| **Mag. Def** | Magic Defense | Mitigates damage from elemental and ethereal spells |
| **Regen** | Recovery Rate | Governs the speed of HP and EP restoration |

### Genetic Potential Tiers

When you report a captured Aniimo, the system evaluates its genetic makeup. This classification is permanent and cannot be altered, making "Perfect" specimens highly sought after for competitive play.

| Quality Tier | Growth Potential | Recommendation |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Common** | Baseline | Use for early-game progression or fusion fodder |
| **Good** | Moderate | Reliable for mid-game questing and exploration |
| **Elite** | High | Suitable for Alpha Boss fights and high-tier PvE |
| **Perfect** | Maximum | The gold standard for endgame and PvP builds |

Beyond elements, Aniimo uses a "Spatial Relation" system that functions like a weighted Rock-Paper-Scissors. This determines how attacks interact based on the creature's physical position in the world.

<Section icon={Swords} title="Combat Roles and Team Synergy">

A balanced party is required for tackling Alpha Aniimo. While it might be tempting to stack four DPS units, the game's mechanics reward a diversified composition that can manage Elemental Power (EP) and the Break meter effectively.

### The Five Combat Roles

| Role | Primary Objective | Key Stat Focus |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **DPS** | Raw damage output | Attack, Regen |
| **Break** | Shredding the Break meter | Attack, Phys. Def |
| **Support** | Buffing allies/Debuffing enemies | Mag. Def, Regen |
| **Regen** | Restoring Elemental Power (EP) | Regen, HP |
| **Healer** | Restoring team HP | HP, Mag. Def |

<Callout type="success" title="Meta Strategy">
The "Regen" role is often overlooked but critical. Creatures like **Wichen** can apply debuffs that restore your team's EP on every hit, allowing your DPS to spam high-damage skills indefinitely.
</Callout>

### Ideal Team Composition for Bossing

For high-level PvE content, we recommend the following 4-slot party structure:

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="The Breaker">
    Start the fight with your Breaker. Their goal is to deplete the enemy's yellow bar as quickly as possible. Use a Breaker with a type advantage for 2x Break efficiency.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="The Support/Regen">
    Cycle in your Regen unit to apply EP-restoration buffs. This ensures your team has enough energy to cast skills without resorting to weak normal attacks.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="The DPS Payload">
    Once the "Break" status is achieved, swap to your primary DPS. This is the time to use your Ultimate attacks, as they will deal quadruple damage (2x from Type + 2x from Break).
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="The Healer (Emergency)">
    Keep a Healer in the final slot to recover from mistakes. Some healers, like Graswing, also provide elemental resistances to keep the team alive during boss Ultimates.
  </Step>
</Steps>
