<Section icon={Target} title="Body Proportions and Silhouette Tuning">

The **aniimo character customization** does not stop at the neck. The body slider system is surprisingly detailed, allowing for asymmetrical-feeling adjustments that provide a sense of weight and athletic build. Unlike standard "thin-to-thick" sliders, Aniimo breaks down the body into segments.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Upper Body Framing">
    Adjust the chest size and waist width to establish the core torso shape. You can also modify upper arm and forearm width independently.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Lower Body Balancing">
    Use the hip width and "appearance" sliders to shift the center of gravity. Thigh and calf width can be tuned to match the upper body's bulk.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Fine Detailing">
    Adjust hand size and overall limb length to ensure the character's proportions match their intended height and role.
  </Step>
</Steps>

| Body Part | Specific Sliders | Usage Recommendation |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Forearms** | Length and Width | **Bold** for heavy armor users |
| **Upper Arms** | Width | Balance with shoulder width |
| **Chest** | Overall Size | Aesthetic choice for outfit fitting |
| **Hips** | Width and Depth | Influences how dresses and pants drape |
| **Calves** | Width | Essential for balancing heavy boot models |
