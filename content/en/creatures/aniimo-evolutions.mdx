<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Branching Evolution Paths and Traits">

One of the most distinct features of the **aniimo evolutions** system is the multi-branch progression. Many creatures in Idyll do not follow a single path; instead, they can adapt into different elemental or functional roles. This allows Pathfinders to tailor their favorite companions to fit specific team gaps, whether they need a high-stagger BREAK specialist or a ranged magic DPS.

For example, a basic Fire-element creature might have the option to evolve into a high-speed physical attacker or a durable tank-style defender. These choices are permanent once the evolution material is consumed, making it vital to review the "Type Matchup" panel before committing.

<Callout type="success" title="Evolution Flexibility">
The January 29 update significantly improved the progression economy. If you evolve a creature and later find a version with a better Personality or higher Ability Values, you can release the old one to recover 80% of the invested Experience Gems.
</Callout>

| Branch Type | Material Focus | Combat Outcome | Exploration Utility |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Aggressive** | Attack-oriented Crystals | Higher Crit Rate, Burst Damage | Faster Ground Movement |
| **Protective** | Defense-oriented Crystals | Shielding Skills, High HP | Resistance to Hazards |
| **Utility** | Support-oriented Crystals | Healing, Buffs, Debuffs | Flight or Tunneling Lv.2 |

Reviewing the traits of your Aniimo before evolution is equally important. Two creatures of the same species can have different personalities. A "Brave" personality might boost physical attack, while a "Calm" one increases magical defense. Use Potential Fruit to nudge these stats if you find a rare Sparkling variant with suboptimal base numbers.

<Section icon={Swords} title="Step-by-Step Evolution Guide">

If you are ready to strengthen your roster, follow these steps to ensure you don't waste rare materials or choose the wrong branching path for your playstyle.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Reach the Level Threshold">
    Focus on your chosen Aniimo and use Experience Gems or active combat to reach the level required for the next stage (usually Level 20 for Gamma). Ensure the creature is in your active roster to maximize passive XP gain.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Gather Regional Materials">
    Check the Aniilog to see which specific materials are needed. For example, creatures found in the Rosetower Wood may require specific flora or crystals only found in that biome.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Compare Branching Options">
    Open the Evolution menu and preview the available branches. Look at the skill changes and elemental shifts. If you lack a Wind-type for flight, choosing a branch that grants "Fly Lv.1" is highly recommended.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Confirm Personality Synergy">
    Ensure your Aniimo's personality matches its intended evolution role. An ATK-boosting personality is wasted on a branch that focuses entirely on healing and support skills.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Trigger the Evolution">
    Consume the required materials to transform your Aniimo. Once evolved, your creature will gain a significant stat boost and often unlock a new Twine Mode ability, such as a faster dash or a higher jump.
  </Step>
</Steps>
