<Section icon={Swords} title="Combat Mechanics and the Twine System">

Combat in **aniimo coop** is more than just issuing commands. The "Twine" system allows Pathfinders to essentially become the Aniimo they have collected. This transformation grants access to the creature's full moveset, including standard attacks, defensive counters, and powerful ultimate abilities.

In a team setting, players can coordinate their Twine transformations to cover different roles. For instance, one player can transform into a tank-class Aniimo like Geoclaw to draw aggro, while the other uses a healer or high-damage dealer like Nova Stage to provide support or burst damage.

### Alpha Boss Strategies

Alpha Bosses are larger, more powerful, and often evolved versions of standard Aniimo found in the wild. They possess unique mechanics, such as shield phases, AoE ground slams, and environmental hazards like freezing or burning fields.

| Boss Type | Common Traits | Best Team Tactic |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Geoclaw (Alpha)** | High defense, Earth/Physical | Use Fire or Dark burst damage |
| **Chirpy (Alpha)** | High mobility, Air/Wind | Coordinate stuns and ranged attacks |
| **Guardian Bosses** | Multiple phases, Shielded | Break shields using ultimate moves |
| **Wild Elite** | Rare variations, High HP | Use status effects and counters |

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Initial Engagement">
    Approach the Alpha Boss together and identify its elemental weaknesses. Use a tank-type Aniimo to initiate the fight and soak up the first wave of attacks.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Shield Breaking">
    Watch for the boss's shield bar. Coordinate your ultimate abilities to "topple" the boss, leaving it vulnerable to massive burst damage for a short duration.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Twine Rotation">
    Rotate your Twine transformations. If one player's Aniimo takes heavy damage, they should disengage and switch to their second slot while the other player maintains pressure.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Capture Attempt">
    Once the boss's health is low (typically under 10%), use an Ultra Pod or higher-tier capture device. The capture chance increases significantly when the boss is toppled or afflicted with status effects.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="Important">
Your human character can only take five hits before fainting. Even while Twined, stay aware of boss attacks that target the player specifically rather than the Aniimo.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Social Systems: Trading and Homeland">

The social aspect of **aniimo coop** extends beyond combat. Players can interact at their personal RV campsites, which serve as mobile social hubs. Here, you can meet other Pathfinders, trade Aniimo, and work together on your Homeland.

### Trading Mechanics

Trading is a core component of the multiplayer experience. The system rewards long-term friendships with reduced costs and better outcomes.

- **Friendship Levels**: The closer you are to a friend, the more Aniimo types you can swap.
- **Illusory Probability**: Higher friendship levels increase the chance of obtaining "Illusory" (shiny/rare) variations during a trade.
- **Trading Costs**: Base trades cost credits, but these are significantly reduced for high-level friends.
